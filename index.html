01

Start with a Top Loader

The top loader is your card's first and most important line of defense. It's a rigid plastic sleeve that prevents any bending or flexing during transit. Without it, even a bubble mailer won't save a card from a careless postal worker or a heavy package dropped on top of it.

Pro Tip

Slide your card into a soft penny sleeve first, then into the top loader. The penny sleeve prevents surface scratching from the plastic edges of the top loader itself — a mistake graders catch immediately.

02

Lock It Between Cardboard

A top loader alone will still move around inside a mailer. The solution is a cardboard sandwich — two pieces of stiff cardboard that grip the top loader on both sides, creating a rigid unit that can't shift, bend, or compress during delivery.

This is the step most beginners skip, and it's the reason cards arrive with dented corners. The cardboard sandwich turns a flexible package into something that functions almost like a rigid mailer at a fraction of the cost.

A card that arrives in perfect condition is the cheapest marketing you'll ever do. That buyer will come back.

03

Seal It in a 4×8 Poly Mailer

Now that your card is rigid and protected, it needs a weatherproof outer shell. A 4×8 poly mailer is the correct size for a single card in a top loader sandwich — snug enough to prevent movement, with enough room to seal cleanly without bulging.

Poly mailers are waterproof, tear-resistant, and lightweight — keeping your shipping cost in the lowest USPS First Class bracket. A padded bubble mailer is overkill for a properly sandwiched card and will cost you more in postage on every single shipment.

Size Confirmed

A standard trading card is 2.5" × 3.5". Inside a top loader with a cardboard sandwich, the package measures roughly 3" × 4" × ¼". A 4×8 poly mailer fits this perfectly with clean sealing room to spare.

Your 90-Second Packing Checklist

  • Slide card into a penny sleeve (optional but recommended for valuable cards)
  • Insert sleeved card into a standard 3×4 top loader
  • Place top loader between two pieces of cardboard cut to size
  • Tape the cardboard edges so nothing can shift
  • Slide the whole sandwich into your 4×8 poly mailer
  • Peel and seal — done
  • Ship USPS First Class for cards under 1 oz (usually $0.68–$1.06)
